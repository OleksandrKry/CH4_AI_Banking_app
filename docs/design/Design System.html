Blackbox · Banking Assistant · Design Foundations v4

Understand your options. Choose with confidence.

Banks carry hundreds of products, each with its own benefits, fees and requirements — confusing for new and experienced customers alike. Getting informed usually means a branch visit, a call queue, or long documents. This app is an on-device AI banking consultant: it guides a customer from a vague need to a confident product choice in one chat — anywhere, anytime — converting more customers while offloading branches and human support.

Dark, premium, Apple-native. #0060AF anchors surfaces as the brand-blue midtone; category identity uses a warm Sanzo-Wada-inspired combination. Every section carries the HIG principle it follows, and everything documented here exists in the shipped SwiftUI code (tokens in Theme.swift, behavior contract in docs/REQUIREMENTS.md).

Use case 1 · Pick a product "I need a card / loan / savings — but I know nothing about this bank." The assistant qualifies the need with a few tappable questions, then recommends concrete products with a clear best pick — quickly, without visiting a branch or contacting anybody.
Use case 2 · Get informed "What's the annual fee? What do I need to apply?" Plain-language questions get relevant, grounded answers — fees, limits, requirements — pulled from the bank's real product data, with a link to the official page to dive deeper.
00

Principles — Apple HIG

Clarity Text stays legible at every size; function always leads over decoration.
Deference Content fills the screen; gradients and glows recede instead of competing.
Depth One sheet rising over chat signals hierarchy — no navigation stack.
Consistency Sheets, chips, capsules — learned platform behavior transfers between screens.
Feedback Every tap responds instantly — a filled chip, the reasoning row, a sheet in motion.
User control Every question offers your-own-answer and Skip; typing always works; swipe dismisses.

HIG
Dedicated dark palette · a single tint color · color never carries meaning alone Three text levels mirror iOS label hierarchy. Blue 400 is the only interactive color, so people learn once what's tappable. Every category hue is paired with a letter glyph and a text tag.

Composition follows 60-30-10. ~60% of any screen is the near-black base; ~30% is elevated surfaces, hairline fills and brand-blue tints (bubbles, panels, glows); the last ~10% is where attention goes — the accent for anything tappable, plus the category hues inside their small marks. Rules
  • Assistant = plain text + accent sparkles; user = brand-tinted bubble, right-aligned.

05

Question card — one question per turn

HIG
Ask only what's needed, one step at a time, always with a way out 3–6 questions are AI-generated once per conversation, then walked one at a time. Every question offers tappable options, a free-text "own answer", and Skip — the user is never funneled.
✦ What will you mostly use the card for?
1 Everyday spending
2 Travel & miles
3 Online shopping
Your own answer… 1/4
Behavior
  • Question text renders as a normal assistant message; the card sits below it.
  • Tapping an option / submitting text / Skip echoes back as a user bubble and advances to the next question — no model call between questions.
  • Progress ("1/4") lives in the own-answer row. Only the latest question is interactive; older ones freeze into history.
  • The same card renders one-off mid-conversation clarifications (no progress label).

HIG
Progressive disclosure — and never a wrong card Cards carry only category, name, one-liner; numbers wait for the sheet. What appears is confidence-gated: a second card shows only when it nearly matches the best hit, so a weak "maybe" never sits next to the right answer.

Display policy (measured)
  • Cards = what the AI actually consulted this turn, max 2, horizontal carousel.
  • First card is always the highest-confidence hit → wears ★ RECOMMENDED (badge + accent border — text and color together).
  • A second card must clear a confidence floor and sit within a small margin of the top hit; calibrated so it removes only wrong cards.
  • Card glow bleeds past the carousel (never clipped into a hard band).

Product sheet — the only modal

HIG
Sheets with detents, truthful data, labeled exits Opens at medium detent, expands in place, swipes to dismiss. Detail rows show the real source strings (fees are never a guessed "Free"; empty fields simply don't render). External links pair a human label with the ↗ convention — never a bare URL.

How it works — on-device pipeline (synced with the product database)

1 · PROFILE — once Occupation + income at first launch; never re-asked. Pre-filters products the user can't get.
▶
2 · SESSION — per conversation Apple's on-device model holds a decision tree of the catalog + two tools. No cloud — nothing leaves the phone.
▶
3 · RETRIEVAL — per need Hybrid search over every product (keywords + semantics; typo- and Indonesian-tolerant). Confidence-scored; weak hits are dropped, not shown.
▶
4 · MEMORY — at finish Apply summarizes the conversation into one line, reused later ("you chose KPR Pembelian last month").
Decision tree ⇄ database (single source: bca-products.json)
C · Cards — 19 products · debit vs credit → use-case & income A · Accounts & investing — 7 · everyday vs growing vs foreign currency L · Loans — 8 · vehicle vs home (KPR) vs cash ? · Everyday services — 13 · transfers, payments, apps (answered directly)
47 products / one retrieval chunk each. The tree, the questionnaire topics, and the category identity all derive from this file — when the catalog changes, update the JSON, re-run scripts/retrieval-eval.sh, and this doc's counts.

12

Handover — extending this system

  • New component? Start from tokens in Theme.swift (they match §01 exactly), hold 44pt targets, use one accent, pair color with text/glyph, and add it here with its HIG rationale.
  • New product or category? Edit bca-products.json only — tree, questionnaire, retrieval, and category identity follow. Re-run scripts/retrieval-eval.sh and keep its accuracy floors green.
  • New conversational behavior? It's a rule in the session instructions or a tool — never app-side routing. Add the requirement to docs/REQUIREMENTS.md with a test.
  • Copy voice: 3–4 sentences, plain language, user's language, no jargon, never a raw error. The headline is the promise: understand your options, choose with confidence.
